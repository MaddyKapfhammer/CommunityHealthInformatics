## Global Health Data Encyclopedia

### Our students have worked together to generate a comprehensive collection of global health data resources.

**Check It Out!**

| Topic | Dataset/base | URL | Description | Unit of Analysis | Noticed Challenges |
| :--: | :--------: | :----: | :---------:  | :-------------: | :---------: |
| **Comprehensive Repositories** | The World Bank | [Check it out](https://data.worldbank.org/) | _Collection of national statistics from hundreds of sources from around the world_ | Country-level & subnational data  | There is more missing data than expected, be flexible and careful of aggregation |
| | UN Data | [Check it out](http://data.un.org/) | _Collection of data and databsases on the national and global scale_ | Global, Country-level, Region, & Sub-Region | This source contains numerous datasets and databases. Best practice is to start with a specific starting point, topic or datatype |
| | IPUMS | [Check it out](https://www.ipums.org/) | _Collection of census microdata for over 100 countries_ | Country-level, international level data | There are a significant number of databases, be sure to approach this source with a specific topic of interest |
| | IFPRI Dataverse | [Check it out](https://www.ifpri.org/publication/ifpri-datasets-dataverse) | _Data repository for surveys and studies about global food systems (1975-present) | Household and community level |  Know exactly what information is essential |
| | AID Data | [Check it out](https://www.aiddata.org/datasets) | _Data repository for aid allocation and effectiveness_ | Country level | May require extensive searching, key words beneficial|
| | World Health Organization | [Check it out](https://www.who.int/data/collections) | _Data repository for numerous sources around the world_ | Country level | Very large repository that one could get lost in if there is not a clear idea of what is needed |
| **U.S. Data** | |||||
|**U.S. Behavorial Risk Factors** | BRFSS | [Check it out](https://www.cdc.gov/brfss/index.html) | _Collects state data about U.S. residents regarding their health-related risk behaviors, chronic health conditions, and use of preventive services over the telephone_ | State level | Intervention can be difficult as individuals refuse to take this telephone survey, leading to less data on health-related activities |
|**U.S. Census Microdata** | American Community Survey | [Check it out](https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs) | _Collected through survey given to 3.5 million random American addresses each year. Information regarding social, economic, demographic, housing, occupation and education_ | Country, state and local level | Researchers should keep in mind that the ACS reflects either 1 year or 5 year data increments. Suggested that 1 year period data should be used for large geographies and 5 year period data should be used for small geographies. |
| **U.S. Health and Nutrition** | NHANES | [Check it out](https://wwwn.cdc.gov/nchs/nhanes/) | _Collect of surveys looking at physical activity and nutrition in youth_ | State and local level | Subjects refusing to take this survey could lead to skewed results |
| **International Household Surveys** | DHS | [Check it out](https://dhsprogram.com/) | _Collection of nationally representative household surveys for over 90 countries | Individual level | This database does not hold much data on AIDS or Malaria indicators in many countries |
| | LSMS | [Check it out](https://microdata.worldbank.org/index.php/catalog/lsms) | _Collected through surveys, censuses, or administrative data collection processes_ | Mostly household level data, some individual level data, business establishments or other facilities | A lot of the datasets have been given to the World Bank by other agencies and are owned by those other organizations. This may limit accessibility or make it more difficult to find data of interest |
| |IHSM | [Check it out](http://catalog.ihsn.org/index.php/catalog) | _Data repository and informal network of international agencies with a variety of household datasets_ | Household, individual level | Large repository requiring basic guiding ideas. Metadata is sometimes not listed |
| **Child Health** | UNICEF MICS | [Check it out](https://mics.unicef.org/) | _Collection of country level surveys for over 362 countries_ | Country, regional, and global level | In order to view past surveys and existing data you must be a registered data user |
| | Global School Based Health Survey | [Check it out](https://www.who.int/teams/noncommunicable-diseases/surveillance/systems-tools/global-school-based-student-health-survey) | _Collection of school based surveys using self-administered questionnaires_ | Individual level, can be aggregated to country level | Biases may be present |
|| Young Lives | [Check it out](https://www.younglives.org.uk/content/use-our-data) | _Colects data on children based on their lives from infancy to early adulthood. Keeps track of how certain conditions such as economic status affect growth_ | Country, international level | This dataset is very specific on only four specific countries |
| **Agriculture** | FAO | [Check it out](http://www.fao.org/statistics/en/) | _Collection of numerous agricultural statistics for countries using questionnaries and various datasets | Global, country, continent level | Filtering required to find data of interest |
| | FEWS | [Check it out](https://fews.net/) | _Collection of data from countries with high food insecurity and risk factors that may lead to famine | Country, regional level | Data is country-specific, variation within the dataset is limited |
| **Nutrition** | Global Dietary Database | [Check it out](https://www.globaldietarydatabase.org/) | _Collection of data on diets around the world to help with nutrition policy | Country, regional level | May need an account to acess some of the datasets |
| | FoodAPS | [Check it out](https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/foodaps-national-household-food-acquisition-and-purchase-survey/) | _U.S. nationally representative survey with very detailed information about food aquisitio for 7 days, including food at home and away from home_ | Household level | Data available in SAS CSV or Stata format. Files can be complex to merge but not all files are needed for all projects |
|**Conflict** | ACLED | [Check it out](https://acleddata.com/#/dashboard) | _Collection of data regarding violence and conflict worldwide_ | Global, regional level | Accessibility limitations |
| | UCDP | [Check it out](https://ucdp.uu.se/) | _Collection of data regarding conflict on a national level (1975-present) | Global, country, level | Some summaries are lacking information for each region of a country |
| **Economics** | Penn World Tables | [Check it out](https://www.rug.nl/ggdc/productivity/pwt/?lang=en) | _Collection of economc national statistics, covering 183 countries from 1950-2019_ | Country level | Data in condensed format|
| | International Monetary fund | [Check it out](https://www.imf.org/en/Data#data) | _Data on macroeconomics and financial sector to help identify economic stability_ | Country level | Can be difficult to download raw data, read what is included in the dataset |
|**Other** | | | | | |
|**Demographics** | UN Population Prospects | [Check it out](https://population.un.org/wpp/) | _Collection of data on the national level from numerous countries globally_ | Country, region and sub-region levels | Possible inaccuracies or missing data |
|**Education** | Barro and Lee Educational Attainment | [Check it out](http://www.barrolee.com/) | _Collection of education data from various countries ranging from 1950-2010_ | Country level |Be clear on the specifics of education that are being studied |
| **Global Disease Burden** | IHME | [Check it out](http://www.healthdata.org/) | _Collection of health statistics from hundreds of countries through surveys_ | Global, country, regional level | Potentially unreliable mortality estimations, graphics have been criticized by epidemiologists |
|**Humanitarian Data** | Humanitarian Data Exchange | [Check it out](https://data.humdata.org/) | _Data repository that has collected datasets on humitarian crises through various organizations_ | International, country level | Dataset is large, be aware of key words and phrases |
|**Labor Conditions** | International Labour Organization | [Check it out](https://www.ilo.org/global/lang--en/index.htm) | _Collection of statistics on working conditions and labor around the world_ | Country level | Identify a topic before investigating | **HIV Law and Policies** | UNAIDS | [Check it out](https://www.unaids.org/en) | _Collection of data regarding HIV and policies in countries around the world | Country level | Data is missing|
|**CDC Expanded** ||||||
|**Child and Adolescent Health** | SHPPS | [Check it out](https://www.cdc.gov/healthyyouth/data/shpps/index.htm) | _Collection of data from surveys on school health policies and practicies_ | State, district, school, classroom level | Diverse data, be aware of the topic at hand |
|**Disabilities** | DHDS | [Check it out](https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/disabilityandhealth/dhds/index.html?CDC_AA_refVal=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cdc.gov%2Fncbddd%2Fdisabilityandhealth%2Fdhds.html) | _Collection of data on adults living with a disability in the United States_ | State level | Data is not highly specific, better for more general analysis |
| **Environmental Health** | NEPHTN | [Check it out](https://ephtracking.cdc.gov/showHome.action) | _Data repository for environmental and health datasets_ | National, state, city level |Only three datasets are available for download |
